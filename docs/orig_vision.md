fabric_ex is a web app called Fabric Stash where people can keep inventory of the fabric they have and use.  my app is in the early stages as an elixir phoenix app.

i want it to be very user friendly. im thinking the main page is like instagram (or what i think instagram used to be) in that it shows you a photo grid of your fabrics (one photo per cut of fabric in your collection). the beauty of your collection is reflected here. this photo grid is meant to display the fabrics in a pleasing way that inspires people to shop their own fabrics stash before aquiring more fabric.

the app lets you input each cut of fabric's details (either manually or by inputting a url) and lets you track its quantity as you use it and archives it if it runs out. would like to add feature of being able to scan a barcode of the fabric to add to the database.

maybe we could generate qr codes for each fabric that you can scan to get fabric details on it in the app. so if i use 3 yards of my medium grey linen, i can scan the qr code and a dialog comes up to ask how much did you use or how much do you have left? also app lets you reorder fabric if its available for purchase somewhere.

one of the main features of the app is that you can query your fabric, like "which natural fabrics do i have enough of to make a longsleeve knit dress in size 16 in a shade of blue?" and since fabric attriibutes are logged, you get back items in ur database that match your query. right now, it is set up to have a filter feature, which i may want to keep, but a search bar seems like a desired feature to have.
